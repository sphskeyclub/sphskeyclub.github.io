Goldfever

About

Goldfever is a lip-syncing and dance competition between the clubs of St. Pete High. It's a SPHS tradition, and it's one that Key participates in each year. It's a great way to get to know more members in the club and to just have fun!

Any Key Club member is welcomed to join Key Club's Goldfever.

Want to participate this year? Learn more about Goldfever 2025 below!

GF 2025

About

Get ready for an unforgettable night of competition, creativity, and community as our four service clubs face off in Goldfever! On January 24, 2025, SPHS will host a high-energy event where the best performers from Interact, Key, Rojans, and Leo come together to compete for the coveted Goldfever awards.

***UPDATE (Jan. 26, 2025)

And the Goldfever 2025 Critics' Choice award goes to..... KEY CLUB!!! Congratulations to all the performers and thank you to everyone who came out to support us! We couldn't have done it without you! Good job everyone!

A Battle For The Ages

Goldfever 2025 isn't just about lip syncing—it's about showcasing the unique styles, teamwork, and talent that each service club brings to the table. With Style Standoff as the overarching theme, each club has chosen a specific style of music to represent, with Key Club setting the stage for a clash between Pop vs. Rock!

Expect a night of electrifying performances as each club showcases their creativity, passion, and dedication. This competition will test every club's ability to bring their music to life in ways you've never seen before.

Key Club: Pop vs. Rock

Our performance will bring the ultimate face-off between two iconic musical genres—Pop vs. Rock.

In one corner, you'll have the infectious, chart-topping hits of modern pop, from artists like Justin Bieber, Bruno Mars, and Beyonce. In the other corner, the rebellious, high-energy power of rock—think Queen, Weezer, and AC/DC. Key Club's performance will be a direct battle of musical styles, highlighting the contrast between pop's catchy melodies and rock's raw, untamed energy. It's a celebration of both genres and the power they have to captivate audiences in very different ways.

The Stakes Are High

This isn't just a fun competition—Goldfever represents the culmination of months of planning, practicing, and collaboration. Each service club has worked tirelessly to craft a performance that represents their club's spirit and their commitment to excellence. From selecting the perfect songs to rehearsing choreography, the effort that goes into these performances reflects the dedication and hard work of each club.

But beyond the competition, Goldfever is also about bringing together the SPHS community in a shared experience. It's a night that celebrates the creativity and camaraderie that service clubs foster. Whether it's through organizing community service events or putting together school-wide activities, the service clubs at SPHS are committed to making a positive impact—and this event is no different.

Why Attend?

Goldfever offers a rare chance to witness a unique blend of performance, teamwork, and school spirit. You won't just be watching a typical lip sync battle—you'll be supporting the service clubs that make a difference in our community every day.

For students, this is a great opportunity to get a first-hand look at what being part of a service club can offer. Goldfever is a perfect way to experience the collaboration, leadership, and fun that define these organizations. If you've been considering joining a service club, attending this event will give you an inside look at what it means to be part of a team that works hard, plays hard, and makes a difference.

How to Get Involved

If Goldfever sparks your interest, now is the perfect time to join. If you're interested in performing we have many resources for you!

You can join the show by signing up for songs using the Google Doc! We'll be posting practice dates and times on the Goldfever Remind and Instagram as well! You'll need to be on the Remind to participate in the performance, the Instagram is more optional but great if you miss practice and need to review choreography (as we post it on there).

***Please understand attendance for practice isn't optional and will have consequences if you miss too often… tech week from January 21-23 is MANDATORY! If you don't attend it, then you will not be able to perform, without exceptions!!

Instagram - @keygoldfever2025

Remind - @keyfever25

Sign Up: after following our Instagram, click the link in our bio to sign up for songs on the Google Doc!

Rushing // Interviews

About

Every year, St. Petersburg High students get the opportunity to rush for a service club.

Rushing is when all the service clubs can promote their clubs by "rushing" the courtyards and handing out their letters. ​For us, that means you can receive a cute paper K!

Each year, any student can apply for Key Club! It is a wonderful club that gives students the opportunity to meet different people and help the people around us. To sign up to be a member of Key Club, there will be applications available for students mid August.

​Then, the clubs hold "interviews". Interviews are usually held in early September, and they will most likely take place after school in Mr. Benton's room (01-223).

Check out some of our best rushing and interview moments over the years below!

Inductions

About

Each year, after interviews, the inductees are welcomed into Key Club at inductions. This night is full of food and entertainment. New members will be officially recognized as members of Key Club International and will receive a pin and certificate along with their membership card. Typically, inductions are held at the Cathedral Church of St. Peter.

This year, we're holding Key Club Inductions at The Cathedral Church of Saint Peter on November 15th (time TBD)! To go, please RSVP here.

Tapping

About

Tapping is one of the greatest traditions only offered at SPHS. Students who have interviewed and finally accepted into the greatest club ever get "tapped" into the club. "Bigs" (members) give baskets filled with goodies and a large K cutout for their "littles" (new members) to wear around their necks and represent the club with pride.
