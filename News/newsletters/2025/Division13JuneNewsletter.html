Summer Service Project Ideas


Service shouldn't have to stop over the summer! Here are some sample summer service project ideas for your clubs:


1. Beach Clean-Up
Organize a beach or park clean-up to protect wildlife, promote environmental awareness, and keep summer spots clean and beautiful.


2. Summer Book Drive
Collect gently used books and donate them to local shelters, libraries, or literacy programs to encourage reading all summer long.


3. Care Packages for the Homeless
Assemble care kits with hygiene products, snacks, and water bottles to support those experiencing homelessness during the hot summer months.


4. DIY Dog Toys for Shelters
Make simple, safe dog toys from old T-shirts and donate them to local animal shelters to help enrich animals’ lives.


5. Popsicles & Positivity
Hand out popsicles and kind notes at a local park or youth center to spread smiles and cool down the community.


6. Lemonade Stand Fundraiser
Host a lemonade stand and donate proceeds to a local charity, children’s hospital, or environmental cause of your club’s choice.

New Year, New Club Goals

As the school year winds down and summer begins, now is the perfect time to start planning for an impactful and exciting Key Club year ahead! Gather your officer board during the summer—even a quick Zoom or group chat check-in can go a long way. Setting clear goals now will help your club hit the ground running once school starts. Strong planning leads to strong service


Here are some goal ideas to guide your planning:

  • Grow Club Membership by 10–15% - to increase membership, you should inform more people about what Key Club is, what we do, the impact we make, the leadership opportunity’s we provide, and participate in your school's club fair and Freshmen Orientation.


  • Boost Social Media Engagement by 10–20% - to improve your social media platforms and spread information, you should post on social media consistently, Inspire people by posting quotes, and promote your social media accounts during club meetings.


  • Increase Member Participation & Service Hours by 20% - to increase service and participation, hold as many service projects as you can (at least 3 a month), partner with other clubs at your school, partner with key clubs from other schools or Kiwanis clubs, and promote it in your club meetings and through social media.


  • Improve Meeting Attendance & Participation - Make meetings fun and informative with icebreakers, games, or even guest speakers (ask your Kiwanis club). Send out reminders and agendas in advance. Have quick check-ins with members (use a Google form) after events to gather feedback.

Key Club International Convention

Celebrate 100 Years of Service!


Get ready for an unforgettable experience at this year’s Key Club International Convention (ICON) in Orlando, Florida — and it’s extra special because we’re celebrating Key Club’s 100th Birthday! ICON is the ultimate celebration of our service year, bringing together Key Clubbers from around the world to connect, lead, and grow.


At ICON, you’ll:

  • Participate in engaging leadership workshops

  • Hear from inspiring speakers

  • Forge lifelong friendships

  • Showcase your club and district achievements

  • Help elect the 2025-2026 International Board

  • Celebrate a century of service and excellence!


Although full registration is closed, you, as members of the Mighty Florida District, have the opportunity to purchase one-day passes to attend ICON!

  • $100 per day (Wednesday, July 2 – Saturday, July 5)

  • Open only to Florida District members

  • Must register by June 13

Important Notes:

  • Attendees must provide their own transportation to the hotel.

  • If you are driving yourself, you must turn in your car keys to a designated Key Club Committee adult member.

  • Please note: You can only be a voting delegate if you attend all four days of the convention.
