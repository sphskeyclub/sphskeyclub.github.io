December 13th Meeting

"If one healthy person's organs could save five lives, is it ethical to sacrifice them?" (Created by Zachary Ratkiewicz!)

Ummm so this one is kind of dark and scary... whoops! However, despite its darkness, 42 of you (56%) said it IS ethical to sacrifice one person to save five lives. I sure hope none of you guys will be my doctor holy cow.

January 31st Meeting

"Is it ethical to repurpose dead bodies in a facility for conservation purposes (fertilizer, saving graveyard space, etc.) if they didn't give consent?" (Created by Brody Whiteside!)

February 21st Meeting

"Would you rather lose all your memories or have everyone lose all their memories of you?" (Created by Joy Santiago!)

This one scares me highkey. Anyways, 38 of our wonderful Keyple friends (66.7%) said that they'd rather LOSE THEIR MEMORIES, instead of having everyone else lose their memories of them. For this one, I agree: I'd probably receive my IB diploma without taking any exams (out of pity). SOUNDS LIKE A DEAL!!! Who's going into a 2 month coma with me!?

March 14th Meeting

"Is it ethical for students to receive additional benefits (scholarships, extra-credit, etc.) for participating in extracurricular activities even when some students do not have the opportunity to participate (due to work, family obligations, etc.)?" (Created by Mrs. Hack!)

This one's probably the most realistic and actually "ethical" question we've asked... probably because this question was created by Mrs. Hack! Anyways, an overwhelming 48 of you (94.1%) said that it IS ethical for students to receive additional benefits! INTERESTING!

April 11th Meeting

"If you could have one (currently existing) non-monetary item/thing that instantly reappears after being used or consumed (no money, gold, or anything that could easily be sold), what would you choose? (Why? Be specific!)" (Created by Zachary Ratkiewicz!)
